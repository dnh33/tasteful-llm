# The Specificity Test — Full Procedure

Run all 5 checks on every sentence before shipping creative/strategic output.

## Check 1: Name Swap

Take the sentence. Replace every proper noun with [PRODUCT]/[COMPANY]. Read it back.

- Still makes sense as a complete thought → **FAIL**
- Becomes incoherent or obviously wrong → **PASS**

**FAIL:** "[PRODUCT] helps teams collaborate more effectively" — works for any product.

**PASS:** "Acme surfaces the three most-replied Slack threads from the last week and drafts a summary your VP can forward" — swap nouns and the sentence needs to know what this product actually does.

## Check 2: Concreteness Test

Does the sentence contain at least one concrete, specific, or falsifiable element? A number, a mechanism, a constraint, a named thing, a concrete metaphor.

- **FAIL:** "We save you time" — no concrete element.
- **PASS:** "Average setup takes 11 minutes" — falsifiable.
- **PASS:** "Works with Figma, Linear, and Notion" — named things.
- **PASS:** "The silence in the room after the demo ended was the loudest thing anyone heard" — concrete metaphor.

## Check 3: Who Cares

Would the reader pay to know this? Does it name or imply a specific person with a specific problem?

- **FAIL:** "Perfect for modern teams" — who? what makes them modern?
- **PASS:** "For eng managers who lose Monday mornings to status meetings" — specific person, specific pain.

## Check 4: Zombie Test

Remove all adjectives and adverbs from the sentence. Does it still say something?

- **FAIL:** "Our powerful, intuitive platform delivers seamless, enterprise-grade solutions" → "Our platform delivers solutions" — says nothing.
- **PASS:** "Paste a URL. Get a summary. Share it in Slack." — nothing to remove, still informative.

## Check 5: So-What Chain

Ask "so what?" three times. Must hit a concrete answer before the chain dies.

- **FAIL:** "We believe in empowering teams" → so what? → "So they can do more" → so what? → "..." — dead end.
- **PASS:** "CI posts a Slack message when a deploy is blocked by a failing migration" → so what? → "On-call engineer knows without checking the dashboard" — concrete on first ask.

## Scoring

- **5/5:** Ship it.
- **3-4/5:** Rewrite, keeping the core idea, adding the missing specificity.
- **0-2/5:** Delete. Start from what is actually true about this specific thing.

## Refine Mode Scoring

When the Operating Mode is Refine (user provided existing text to improve):

- **5/5:** Ship as-is. Do not touch.
- **3-4/5:** The sentence is alive but imperfect. Preserve it. Present the failed check(s) and offer a tightened alternative — e.g., "This sentence fails the Name Swap check. Here's a version that passes: [alternative]. Keep or swap?" Default: keep the original.
- **0-2/5:** Rewrite. The sentence is genuinely hollow regardless of who wrote it.

The user's existing prose that clears 3/5 has earned the right to survive unless they choose to sharpen it. New prose generated by this skill has no such right — it must hit 5/5 or die.
