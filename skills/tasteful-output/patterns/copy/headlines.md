# Heading-Level Slop — C1, C2, C4

Patterns where headlines, subheadlines, and mission statements say nothing specific.

---

## C1: Everything Headline

**Slop:** "The All-in-One Platform for Modern Teams"

**Why it's slop:** This headline describes every B2B SaaS product. "All-in-one" means you don't know what to emphasize. "Modern teams" means you don't know who your customer is.

**Fix:** Name the outcome and the person: "Engineering managers: stop writing status reports." Or name the mechanism: "One Slack command. Every sprint metric. Every Monday at 9am."

---

## C2: Subheadline Repeat

**Slop:** Headline: "Automate Your Workflow." Subheadline: "Our powerful platform helps you streamline and automate complex business processes."

**Why it's slop:** The subheadline's job is to add new information. This one restates the headline with more words and less clarity. The reader has been told the same thing twice and knows nothing.

**Fix:** Headline = the promise. Subheadline = the proof or mechanism. "Automate your deploy pipeline." / "Push to main. Acme runs your test suite, builds the artifact, and deploys to staging. You review in the morning."

---

## C4: Mission Statement Void

**Slop:** "We're on a mission to transform how teams collaborate, enabling organizations to achieve more with less."

**Why it's slop:** Every company is on a mission to transform something. "Achieve more with less" is the literal definition of a tool. This sentence cannot be falsified, attributed to a specific company, or remembered.

**Fix:** "We build the tool that replaces your team's weekly status meeting." Specific, memorable, falsifiable.
