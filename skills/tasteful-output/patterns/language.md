# Language Patterns — L1 through L12

12 patterns where word choice creates slop. Organized by root disease.

## Modifier Diseases (L1, L3, L6)

When output stacks adjectives, piles synonyms, or substitutes enthusiasm for information.

- **L1: Adjective Crutch** — Stacking modifiers where one fact would do
- **L3: Thesaurus Parade** — Three synonyms in a row that all mean the same thing
- **L6: Enthusiasm Proxy** — Your excitement replacing the reader's interest

Full examples and fixes: @language/modifiers.md

## Information Voids (L2, L5, L10, L11)

When sentences carry zero information — openers that clear their throat, closers that say nothing, appeals to consensus that skip the argument.

- **L2: Fortune-Cookie Close** — Dramatic ending that could close any pitch about anything
- **L5: Breathless Opener** — "In today's fast-paced..." throat-clearing before the point
- **L10: Consensus Appeal** — "Everyone knows..." to skip proving the claim
- **L11: Temporal Urgency Fake** — "Now more than ever" (always true, never meaningful)

Full examples and fixes: @language/voids.md

## Claim Evasion (L4, L7, L8, L9, L12)

When output avoids committing to any concrete, falsifiable claim — hedging, abstract verbs, false choices, and hollow contrasts.

- **L4: Weasel Hedge** — "Helps you potentially reduce some friction"
- **L7: Empower/Unlock/Leverage** — Abstract verbs replacing literal descriptions
- **L8: False Binary** — "Either the old way or our product" hiding all alternatives
- **L9: Anthropomorphized Product** — "Our platform understands" (software doesn't understand)
- **L12: Hollow Contrast** — "Not just X, but Y" where Y restates X

Full examples and fixes: @language/evasion.md
