# Copy Patterns — C1 through C8

8 patterns where headlines, tone, and rhetorical choices create slop. Organized by root disease.

## Heading-Level Slop (C1, C2, C4)

When headlines, subheadlines, and mission statements try to describe everything and end up describing nothing.

- **C1: Everything Headline** — "The All-in-One Platform for Modern Teams"
- **C2: Subheadline Repeat** — Subheadline restates headline with more words
- **C4: Mission Statement Void** — "On a mission to transform how teams collaborate"

Full examples and fixes: @copy/headlines.md

## Voice & Tone Slop (C3, C5, C6, C7, C8)

When tone substitutes for substance — fake enthusiasm, passive evasion, fabricated dialogue, unearned emotional intimacy.

- **C3: "Great Question!"** — Evaluating the question instead of answering it
- **C5: Passive Explainer** — "It should be noted that..." passive hedge
- **C6: Imaginary Dialogue** — "You might be thinking..." with a weak strawman
- **C7: Permission Grant** — "It's okay to feel overwhelmed" (you're software)
- **C8: Aspirational Second Person** — "You deserve..." flattery replacing argument

Full examples and fixes: @copy/tone.md
